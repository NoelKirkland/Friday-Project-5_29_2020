About Me

I am just your normal 27 your old guy. I was born and raised in Portland Oregon, and went to highschool at Grover Cleveland High. Right after highschool I packed up and moved to Chicago Illinois for a year. I never planned on living there for good, I guess I just wanted to experience independence after living with my family my whole life. After Chicago I moved back to Portland where I rented my own apartment, worked as a line cook, and dabbled in community college a little bit. When I was 23 years old I started feeling a little restless again living in Portland and wanted to leave. I thought to myself "which city will have the best weather in all of America?" and I quickly concluded that I would move to San Diego California.

In California I finally landed my first real career job working at a tech strart-up. This is where I was first introduced to coding which piqued my interest immediately, but I didn't really do much with. After a couple of years spent down in San Diego I moved back up to Portland where I landed another job at another tech. Again, people were coding there, and it really intrigued me, but I just didn't have the know-how to do much myself

My Background

I graduated from highschool in 2011 and, after a gap year living in Chicago, IL, I came back to Portland and enrolled at Portland Community College. I didn't have a clue what I wanted to do at that point so I just took a lot of prerequisites and a few electives that sounded interesting. With college being so expensive I decided that it was irresponsible to continue with college when I have no real sense of what I wanted to do or to major in.

I have worked many jobs but the last two jobs I had were in the realm of what I would like to do. The last jobs I had were both at tech start-ups. At the first of these two I worked as an Implementation Specialist for a digital marketing company. As an Implementation Specialist I would add content like copy, images, links, lists, and resorces into a content management system (or CMS for short). This would populate on the front end, displaying all of the content the client provided as a beautiful personalised website.

The second of my last two jobs I worked as a Fraud Prevention Specialist for a popular vacation rental company. As a Fraud Prevention Specialist I would scan through high risk vacation reservations to try to identify whether or not the reservation was created using a stolen credit card. If I was able to conclusively identify one of these reservations I would cancel it, refund the card, and then reach out to the authorities to turn over what I have found.

There were certain coding elements required by these jobs, but nothing very specialized. But, during the times I was required to code, I would really enjoy it. I had tried to use a few languages to streamline my workflow and the workflow of my team but I would always encounter obstacles and barries that I simply did not have the know-how needed to overcome. I enrolled in the Epicodus coding bootcamp in order to obtain the knowledge necessary to solve problems, streamline workflows, and build my own projects from scratch. Once I graduate I look forward to landing a job as a programmer!
